Product Strategy
Retail Lending Products — Piramal Finance (Alt Uni Capstone)
☆ North Star

Scale Retail AUM: ₹65 Cr → ₹90 Cr by FY28

Target: Tier 2/3 cities, first-time borrowers

⚙ What I built

Two product avenues: Co-branded Credit Cards + Gold Loans

Digital customer journeys + GTM across full funnel

Context: Piramal Finance (PF) is a retail-led NBFC with ₹65 Cr AUM (55% retail). The goal was to identify two growth avenues — unsecured (credit cards) and secured (gold loans) — and design digital-first products targeting underserved Bharat segments to reach ₹90 Cr AUM.

Avenue 1 — Co-branded Credit Cards (Unsecured)

Market Opportunity: India's credit card industry grew 11.64% in 9 months (Aug 2023 – May 2024, RBI data). The sector has seen a 20% CAGR over 5 years, yet only 3% of India's population holds a formal credit card — a massive whitespace.

Where to play: Tier 2 & 3 cities (65% of current cards concentrated in Tier 1), self-employed professionals (SMEs, freelancers), and early jobbers aged 18–25. Target income band: ₹2.5–5 Lakhs. Average ticket size: ₹4,000–5,000.

Competitive benchmark: Bajaj Finserv, Tata Capital, and Aditya Birla Capital already offer co-branded cards. 7 of top 10 NBFCs do not — first-mover advantage exists for PF.

Voyageur — AI Trip Planner
◎ Outcome

Full product shipped in 1 day, $0 cost

4/4 milestones completed · 2 interfaces · 2 memory layers

◈ My Role

AI PM — product vision, feature prompts (as PRD), tech stack decisions, 4-milestone roadmap, E2E testing & debugging

Problem: Planning a trip takes 3–4 hours scattered across Google, TripAdvisor, blogs, and budget calculators. Travelers know what they want in plain English — they just need someone to organize it.

Opportunity: An AI assistant that understands natural language, generates structured itineraries, and remembers preferences across sessions.

User Persona

Shiv, 26 — MBA student. Pain: 3–4 hrs per trip plan. Goal: personalized itinerary in minutes. Constraints: budget-conscious, vegetarian.

Product Roadmap
M1 · Core MVP ✅
Describe trip → structured day-by-day plan with costs in INR (terminal)
M2 · Conversational ✅
Multi-turn chat — refine itinerary through natural conversation
M3 · Persistent Memory ✅
Dual memory layer — Mem0 (cloud) + ChromaDB (local offline fallback)
M4 · Web Interface ✅
Streamlit web app with visual cards — no terminal needed
Prompt Engineering & Vibe Coding
M1 · Setup & One-Shot Planner
Build Voyageur using Python, Groq (Llama 3.3 70B), and Pydantic. Accept plain-English travel requests, generate a structured trip brief and day-by-day itinerary with INR costs. Handle malformed JSON with retry logic. Keep the AI layer modular for future enhancements.
M2 · Conversational Refinement
Transform into a multi-turn assistant. Maintain conversation history so users can refine plans, ask follow-ups, and request alternatives in natural language. Keep itineraries internally consistent. End each session with a final consolidated itinerary.
M3 · Memory with Mem0 + ChromaDB
Add persistent memory using Mem0 (cloud) and ChromaDB (local fallback). Load preferences at session start, personalise responses. At session end, extract meaningful signals — dietary needs, budget, travel style. Include a debug command to inspect stored memories.
M4 · Shareable Web Frontend
Build a Streamlit web interface — no terminal needed. Support itinerary generation, conversational refinement, and memory-enabled interactions. Display plans in a structured visual format. Integrate cleanly with existing backend without modifying planning, chat, or memory layers.
